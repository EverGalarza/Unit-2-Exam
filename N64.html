The Nintendo 64, released in 1996, was a groundbreaking console known for its innovative 3D graphics and iconic controller with an analog stick. It introduced classics like Super Mario 64 and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, setting a standard for 3D gaming experiences that still resonate with gamers today.
